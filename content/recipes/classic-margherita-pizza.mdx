This is the pizza that started it all for me. The first time I had a real Margherita in Naples, I understood what all the fuss was about — and I've been chasing that perfect bite ever since.

## Tips for Success

- **Use a pizza stone or steel** if you have one. Preheat it in the oven for at least 30 minutes at the highest temperature your oven can go.
- **Don't overload the toppings.** A true Margherita is about balance — a little sauce, a little cheese, a few basil leaves.
- **Wet mozzarella is the enemy.** Pat your fresh mozzarella dry with paper towels before slicing, or you'll end up with a soggy crust.

## Instructions

1. **Activate the yeast.** In a small bowl, combine warm water (110°F / 43°C) and yeast. Let sit for 5 minutes until foamy.

2. **Make the dough.** In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.

3. **Knead.** Turn dough onto a floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. The dough should spring back when poked.

4. **First rise.** Place dough in an oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let rise in a warm spot for 1-2 hours until doubled in size.

5. **Shape.** Divide dough into 4 equal balls. On a semolina-dusted surface, stretch each ball into a 10-inch round. Use your hands — don't use a rolling pin, which deflates the bubbles.

6. **Preheat.** Place a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet in the oven and preheat to 500°F (260°C) or the highest setting for at least 30 minutes.

7. **Assemble.** Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce on each dough round. Tear mozzarella pieces and distribute evenly.

8. **Bake.** Slide pizzas onto the hot stone (one at a time) and bake for 8-12 minutes until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling.

9. **Finish.** Remove from oven, top with fresh basil leaves, drizzle with a little olive oil, and serve immediately.

Enjoy your taste of Naples!
