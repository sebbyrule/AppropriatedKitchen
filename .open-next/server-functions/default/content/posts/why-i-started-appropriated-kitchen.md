Every great kitchen has a story. This one starts with a question: **why do we cook the way we do?**

I grew up in a house where food was love. My grandmother never used a recipe — she cooked by feel, by taste, by memory. A pinch of this, a handful of that, and somehow it was always perfect. When I asked her how she did it, she'd smile and say, "That's *my* way."

Years later, living in my own apartment with a tiny kitchen and even tinier budget, I realized something. The recipes I was finding online were either:

1. Overcomplicated (13 ingredients for Tuesday night dinner? No thanks.)
2. Watered down (where's the flavor?)
3. Gatekept behind "secret" techniques

So I started doing what my grandmother did — taking the food I loved and making it *my way*. 

## The Philosophy

**Appropriated Kitchen** isn't about cultural appropriation — it's about *culinary appreciation*. It's the belief that great food belongs to everyone, and that the best recipes are the ones you make your own.

- **Take what you love.** Find dishes that excite you.
- **Learn the why.** Understand the technique, not just the steps.
- **Make it yours.** Adjust, experiment, break the rules.

## What to Expect

This blog will have:

- **Recipes** that are tested, photographed, and written with personality
- **Nutritional info** for every recipe (because eating well matters)
- **Printable recipe cards** so you can cook without scrolling
- **Honest opinions** about what worked, what didn't, and what I'd change next time

## Your Food, My Way

That's not just a tagline. It's an invitation. Take these recipes, make them your own, and let me know how it goes.

Welcome to the kitchen.
